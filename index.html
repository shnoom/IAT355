The Fast Fashion Impact

In 2023, the fast fashion industry was estimated to be worth $122.98 billion and is expected to reach $197.5 billion by 2028. At its core, fast fashion is the production of low-quality clothes that mimic popular-styles and trends sold at rock-bottom prices. This makes it easy for consumers to purchase stylish items, which promotes frequent shopping and quick turnovers in trends and fashion collections. The main goal of fast fashion is to essentially get people to spend more money on clothes they use for shorter times

Today, we will be examining the social, environmental and ethical impact of the 5 most dominant fast fashion brands of today, Shein, Forever21, Zara, H&M and Uniqlo.

Let’s zoom out and see where these brands factories are located to see if we can identify any patterns.

THE HUMAN COST

THE HANDS BEHIND THE CLOTHES

Cheap clothing depends on cheap labour. Behind every low-priced, ‘steal’ clothing in fast fashion, there are workers who are sacrificing their health, time, and life to make that possible. Long hours, unsafe working conditions, and low wages are often normalized within these production systems, allowing brands to maintain high output at minimal cost.

THE ENVIRONMENTAL COST

HOW THE PLANET PAYS THE PRICE

Fast fashion has a heavy environmental footprint. Producing garments consumes large amounts of water, especially during material processing and dyeing, while manufacturing and global distribution contribute to rising carbon emissions. Because these clothes are often worn only briefly, many are quickly discarded, adding to the increasing volume of textile waste in landfills. As a result, fast fashion’s speed and scale come at a significant cost to the planet.
