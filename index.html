The Fast Fashion Impact

Dataset from Kaggle

Analyzing the impact these 5 fast fashion brands, Shein, Forever21, Zara, H&M and Uniqlo, have on the economic, social and enviromental state of the world.

1. Far From the Storefront

Where fast fashion production is located

Fast fashion may be sold in polished malls and online storefronts, but its production network stretches far beyond where most people shop. This map reveals where our featured brands manufacture across the globe. A strong cluster appears across Asia, where brands rely on established manufacturing networks, lower labour costs, and weaker worker protections to produce clothing at scale.

1. The Volume of "New"

Average monthly textile production of each brand in tonnes

Every month, these five brands alone churn out thousands of tonnes of textile. Shein leads, producing over 325,000 tonnes of clothing every 30 days.

2. Water consumption

Annual water usage in million liters.

To produce this much, the water requirement is staggering. We compared the annual water usage of a retailer to a single Canadian household. On this scale, the household usage of 81,395 litres is practically invisible.

The red line at the bottom represents 1 year of life for a family of four. The bars represent 1 year of production.

3. The Affordability Crisis

We calculated the daily wage of a worker versus the price of a single item they produce. In almost every case, a worker cannot afford a single piece of clothing with their entire day's pay.

For a Zara worker to buy one $20 item, they would need to work for almost 4 days, spending nothing else.

This is the cost without even taking in to account the number of hours a worker works in a day.
